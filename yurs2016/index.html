About YURS 2016

The Yale Undergraduate Research Showcase (YURS) is more than just a research conference. It is a discourse between and among the undergraduates of Yale and the rest of the Yale community, encouraging students to pursue their passions for research and facilitate the communication of ideas across all disciplines.

The Yale Undergraduate Research Association (YURA), as one of our founding missions, believes that a strong research community is paramount to enable the undergraduates at Yale to positively influence the world of research, both in the present and in their future careers. The skills and passions they learn now in the lab and in their studies will translate to the rest of their lives as they leave Yale and continue onto graduate school, medical school, industry, and beyond, culminating in the pursuit of their own research interests independently as principal investigators. It is our hope that by fostering such a strong research community among the Yale undergraduates, YURA will promote the inspiration and connections necessary to strengthen these ties to research, and ultimately influence students to continue in research and contribute to the pursuit of knowledge.

YURS furthers this goal by giving a voice to the undergraduates who conduct research at Yale. We promote the growth of our community uniquely through interdisciplinary communication. Our showcase exposes students to all methods of problem solving each discipline has to offer, from anthropology to political science to mathematics. Yale prides itself on its liberal arts, and YURS highlights this strength by illustrating that sharing concepts between the sciences, humanities, and social sciences fosters more impactful communication of ideas than connections solely within one discipline, broadening the minds of each researcher and inspiring creativity and innovation.

Students present their research in an engaging poster session in the Nave of Sterling Memorial Library.
